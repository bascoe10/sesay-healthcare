Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about Sesay Healthcare Services LLC

1. What services do you provide?

We offer in-home support such as personal care, companionship, meal preparation, light housekeeping, mobility assistance, transportation to appointments, and respite care for families. All services are non-clinical and focused on daily living support.

2. Who can receive care?

We support seniors, adults with disabilities, individuals recovering after hospitalization (non-medical assistance), and anyone who needs help with daily routines or household tasks.

3. Do you provide medical or nursing services?

No. We are a non-skilled home care agency, which means we do not provide medical, nursing, or clinical services. Our caregivers assist with daily living, safety, comfort, and companionship.

4. How do you match caregivers with clients?

We consider your loved one's needs, personality, preferences, and schedule. Our goal is to create a comfortable, trusting relationship between the caregiver and the client.

5. Are your caregivers trained?

Yes. All caregivers receive ongoing training in personal care, safety, communication, and client dignity. They also undergo background checks and screenings before joining our team.

6. How quickly can services begin?

Most families can begin care within a few days. In urgent situations, we do our best to accommodate same-week or next-day starts depending on caregiver availability.

7. What areas do you serve?

We proudly serve: Philadelphia • Delaware County • Montgomery County • Bucks County. Visit our Service Areas page for more detail.

8. Do you offer part-time or full-time care?

Yes. We offer flexible scheduling — from a few hours a day to full-day support. Care plans are customized to your family's needs.

9. How much does home care cost?

Rates vary based on the level of care, hours needed, and location. We provide transparent pricing during your care consultation so you know exactly what to expect.

10. How do I get started?

Simply contact us to schedule a care consultation. We'll discuss your needs, answer your questions, and create a personalized care plan for your loved one. You can also call us directly at (603) 800-1813.
